ML-based Microarchitecture Simulation Made Practical

Project Description: Microarchitecture simulators, which play a crucial role in evaluating and advancing microarchitecture research during design space exploration (DSA), take a notoriously long time. Different optimizations with dissimilar abstraction levels have been introduced to speed up the simulations. However, each level of abstraction would sacrifice simulation accuracy for a better speed. Recently, ML-based simulations have emerged as a promising solution because it is both fast and accurate. Unfortunately, current ML-based simulators still face mounting challenges, e.g., prolonged preprocessing time, lack of architectural details, and limited feature representations, which makes them impractical. In this proposal, we plan to dismantle all these limitations so that ML-based microarchitecture simulation can be practical.
